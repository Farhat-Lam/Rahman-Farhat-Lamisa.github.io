Aerial Image Segmentation for Building Detection Using Deep Convolutional Neural Networks

Semantic segmentation of aerial images to detect buildings and compute their areas with a deep CNN, achieving 91.3% accuracy and 90.0% F1â€‘score on the INRIA dataset.

Read full abstract & view images

Image segmentation for building detection has applications in a variety of sectors, including urban planning, mapping, disaster management, and relief, among others. Many different techniques have been applied to extract building features and among them, using a convolutional neural network is the state-of-the-art method in recent years.

Recent data science and remote sensing technologies make automated building detection possible, significantly decreasing manual labor and contributing to dynamic urban monitoring. However, it has been a challenging task since many years to detect buildings from aerial images due to a wide range of parameters that can influence decision-making.

In this study, a technique has been proposed that uses deep learning to perform semantic segmentation to identify the buildings from aerial images. The system also performs additional steps to calculate the areas of the individual buildings from the images.

INRIA dataset has been used in this study and the best performing network architecture was chosen after conducting a series of experiments on different architectures. This served as the baseline network architecture. To create an optimal system, a number of tasks were carried out in addition to a large number of experiments and the outcomes were analyzed quantitatively.

Our proposed method achieved a 91.3% accuracy and a 90.0% F1score on the INRIA dataset. The system then uses the segmented output from the network to determine the area of the buildings.
